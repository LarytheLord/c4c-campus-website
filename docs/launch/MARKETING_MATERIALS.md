## Brand Guidelines

### Tone & Voice

**DO**:
- Be inclusive and welcoming
- Assume no prior knowledge
- Be direct and action-oriented
- Show passion for the mission
- Use collaborative language ("we," "let's," "together")
- Celebrate community contributions
- Admit limitations honestly

**DON'T**:
- Use jargon without explanation
- Be preachy or judgmental
- Oversell or make false promises
- Use exclusionary language
- Center the technology over people
- Ignore constructive criticism
- Pretend to be perfect

### Taglines & Key Phrases

**Primary**: "Learn AI for Animal Liberation"
**Secondary**: "The infrastructure for animal advocacy"
**Tertiary**: "Education meets acceleration"

**Short phrases**:
- "Designed for liberation"
- "By advocates, for advocates"
- "From learning to impact"
- "Practical tools. Real people. Global movement."
- "No barriers. No cost. No excuses."

### Visual Branding

**Colors**:
- Primary: C4C Green (#2D7B3F)
- Secondary: C4C Gold (#D4A574)
- Accent: Liberty Red (#C41E3A)
- Neutral: Dark Gray (#333333)

**Fonts**:
- Headlines: [Bold, sans-serif]
- Body: [Clean, readable sans-serif]
- Code: [Monospace for technical content]

**Imagery**:
- Show real people (diverse, authentic)
- Show community (not isolation)
- Show impact (before/after, outcomes)
- Use animals (respectfully, empoweringly)
- Global representation (different countries, contexts)

### Grammar & Style

**Capitalization**:
- "C4C Campus" (always capitalized)
- "Bootcamp," "Hackathons," "Accelerator" (capitalized when referring to programs)
- "animal advocacy," "animal liberation" (lowercase)

**Format**:
- Use contractions ("you're," "we're," "don't")
- Use active voice
- Keep sentences short and scannable
- Use bullet points for lists
- Link important terms on first mention
